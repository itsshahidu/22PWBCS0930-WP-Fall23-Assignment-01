Travel Story of Peshawar, Pakistan

Overview | Myself and Peshawar | Things to Do in Peshawar | Places to Eat & Drink | Places to Stay |

Overview

Peshawar, the capital of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province in Pakistan, is one of the oldest living cities in the world. It has a rich history dating back over 2,500 years, and has been ruled by various empires over the centuries, including the Greeks, Persians, Mughals, and British. Peshawar is a cultural melting pot, and its people are known for their hospitality and warmth. The city is home to a diverse population, including Pashtuns, Sikhs, Hindus, and Christians.

Myself and Peshawar

I love Peshawar for its rich history, culture, and people. I am fascinated by the city's ancient past, and I enjoy exploring its many historical sites. I also love the city's vibrant culture and its delicious food.

Top Things to Do in Peshawar

Islamia College Peshawar: Islamia College Peshawar, the symbol of academic excellence is rich in history. Its building, which is the best embodiment of Muslim civilization in south Asia and its lush green lawns attract visitors from all over the country and abroad. It is not only the culminating point of the Aligarh Movement, it is in fact the beautiful combination of Aligarh and Deoband Schools of Thought. Being the breeding ground of the Pashtun intelligentsia, this great seat of learning is the place, where diamonds are skimmed since 1913. Moreover, Islamia College Peshawar was destined to become mother institution of the three universities and several colleges on its campus. The credit of this goes to Sir Sahibzada Abdul Qayyum, who is commonly called The Sir Sayyed of the Frontier and his sincere colleagues, who founded Islamia College Peshawar in 1913.
Bala Hisar Fort: This ancient fort is one of the most iconic landmarks in Peshawar. It offers stunning views of the city and the surrounding countryside.
Khyber Pass: This mountain pass is a strategic route that has been used by armies and traders for centuries. It is a beautiful and historic place to visit.
Peshawar Museum: This museum houses a vast collection of artifacts from Peshawar's rich history. It is a great place to learn about the city's past and culture.
Sethi Mosque: This beautiful mosque is one of the oldest in Peshawar. It is known for its intricate architecture and its peaceful atmosphere.

Best Places to Eat and Drink in Peshawar

Namak Mandi: This famous food market is a must-visit for foodies. It is known for its wide variety of dishes, including kebabs, curries, and biryani.
Kababish Restaurant: This popular restaurant is known for its delicious kebabs. It is a great place to try some of Peshawar's famous cuisine.
Peshawar Restaurant: This restaurant serves a variety of traditional Pakistani dishes. It is a great place to try some of Peshawar's local cuisine.
Bukhara Restaurant: This restaurant serves a variety of Central Asian dishes. It is a great place to try some of the unique flavors of Peshawar.
